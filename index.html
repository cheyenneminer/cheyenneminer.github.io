I am currently a student in the School of Journalism and Communications at the University of Oregon, pursuing a degree in Journalism with minors in both Women's and Gender Studies and Multimedia Design. I spend a lot of my time at my part time barista/standing manager job at Full City Coffee Roasters in Eugene.

Being involved in activities on campus and in the surrounding community is important to me. I am a member of a student group called UO Hack. UO Hack is a computer programming group focusing on HTML and CSS coding. I am also on staff at Oregon Voice, a student run publication for student writing and art. Currently, I am a volunteer for Safe Ride at University of Oregon Women's Center. A tool I often use to further my community involvement is my passion for Yoga. After I graduated from Junction City High School I was a volunteer yoga instructor for the women's soccer team. My passion for yoga also inspired me to start my blog; Let's Get Twisted, to share my yoga experiences with the internet community.

In addition to my strong journalism and multimedia background, I am versed in multiple areas of science including; Chemistry and Biology. I have completed about one third of a Human Physiology degree.

English is my primary spoken language, but I have studied both Chinese and Spanish.

I am interested in the sharing of information across multiple platforms, photojournalism, graphic design, computer programming and all things epic in the field of journalism.
